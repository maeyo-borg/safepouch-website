Our Story

Give the
room back.

We didn't build SafePouch in a boardroom. We built it as students who couldn't focus in class and friends who watched half a concert through a screen. It's the simple object we wish we'd had — one that keeps you in the real room instead of the digital one.

Our mission

Staying in touch with reality.

In class, the phone always won. On nights out — concerts, shows, just being with friends — we kept catching ourselves living the moment through a screen instead of actually being there. It was never about willpower. The problem was having the thing in your pocket at all.

SafePouch takes it out of the loop. Not confiscated, not tracked — just locked while it matters, and back in your hand the second it doesn't. The goal isn't less technology. It's more presence: being where your feet are, with the people in front of you.

Our story

It started with us.

This didn't start as a business idea. It started with us realizing how much we'd quietly traded away to a screen — focus in school, and being fully there with friends at the shows we'd looked forward to all week.

So we built the thing we wished existed: a locking pouch tough enough for a school day and reliable enough for a sold-out show. People keep their own phones — they just get to be somewhere again. Today it's trusted across schools and events, and every pouch can be customized to a district's or school's colors, logo, or mascot.

What we stand for

Simple by design

No apps, no accounts, no daily power struggles. One object that quietly does the job, every period and every show.

Trust, not surveillance

Students and guests keep their own devices. No tracking, no data collection — just a locked pouch they hold onto all day.

Presence over screens

A classroom that's device-free for six hours is a different classroom. A show that's device-free for two hours is a different show.
